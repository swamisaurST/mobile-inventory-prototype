Field Central · O&M
Last week, with you

Topics we covered.

  • Field tech inventory workflow today Manual, email-driven hand-offs from tech to branch manager — the pattern we're here to replace.
  • Branch manager as the bottleneck Joe and Pete personally execute every transfer. Mobile self-serve must reduce that load, not add to it.
  • Reservation expectations & gaps Today "set aside" lives on a sticky note in the warehouse. We need it system-native, visible to the tech, and trustworthy enough to act on.
  • Approval & audit trail needs Tech-initiated actions need manager approval above a threshold. Every action leaves a trace — who requested, who approved, when.
  • Cycle counts as the bigger unlock Phase 2 territory, but it shapes how we model inventory in Phase 1 — without count reconciliation, bulk inventory won't scale.
  • Beta candidacy + permissions scope Internal techs need standard mobile licenses (not Community Portal). Spruce is moving on this — pilot can start in September.
Field Central · O&M
What we learned

How Spruce operates today.

  • Order-per-job today, not bulk inventory Reservation matters less in 2026, but acutely as Spruce scales toward the 2027 bulk model. We're designing for where you're going, not just where you are.
  • NJ + SoCal warehouses · Houston drop-ships Two operating models — internal fleet with branch warehouses vs. third-party with direct-to-site. Both need solving, but Phase 1 leads with the warehouse model.
  • Branch managers (Joe, Pete) do every transfer manually Single point of failure in the current workflow. Mobile self-serve directly relieves the bottleneck without removing the manager's authority.
  • 10-day SLA first visit · 30-day to close Reservation TTL has to fit inside job execution windows. Too tight and reservations expire mid-job; too loose and inventory gets ghost-held forever.
  • Going on Sitetracker mobile mid-May 2026 Beta timing lines up. The pilot starts when your techs land on the new mobile experience — September is the right window.
  • Bulk inventory ambition: 2027+, gated on cycle counts Cycle counts is the Phase 2 unlock. Without physical-count reconciliation, the rest of the inventory roadmap can't scale to bulk.

New this week

Built since we last spoke.

01 · Swap & remove

Replace an asset in one transaction.

Tech taps Install. Picks the old asset to swap out. New asset goes in, old asset comes off the site. No duplicates, no manual back-end work for the branch manager.

02 · Request from warehouse

Out of stock on the truck? Request it.

Tech sees warehouse stock read-only, submits a request, and tracks it: Requested → Approved → In transit → Received. No more "did Joe see my email?"
